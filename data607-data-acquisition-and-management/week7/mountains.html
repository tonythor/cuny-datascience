Mountaineering Books

The Wildest Dream: The Biography of George Mallory

Authors: Peter Gillman, Leni Gillman

This biography delves into the life of George Mallory, the mountaineer who famously claimed to want to climb Mount Everest 'because it's there'. Mallory and his climbing partner, Andrew Irvine, disappeared during their 1924 expedition, leaving it a mystery as to whether they were the first to reach the summit.

Year Published: 2000

K2: Life and Death on the World's Most Dangerous Mountain

Authors: Ed Viesturs, David Roberts

This book provides a detailed account of the history and allure of K2, the world's second-highest mountain, which has a reputation as one of the most challenging and deadly. Viesturs, one of the world's premier high-altitude mountaineers, interweaves his own experiences on K2 with the stories of those who have faced the mountain's challenges.

Year Published: 2009

Deborah: A Wilderness Narrative

Authors: David Roberts, Doug Scott

This account details the gripping adventure of the first ascent of the East Face of Mount Deborah in Alaska. The duo faced daunting challenges, and the narrative presents not only the physical challenges of the climb but also the psychological and emotional hurdles they had to overcome.

Year Published: 1970
